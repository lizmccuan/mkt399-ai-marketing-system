# Best Practices Summary

## 1. Website / UX / Conversion Best Practices
- Make websites simple, intuitive, and easy to navigate
- Reduce clutter and guide users toward one clear action per page
- Use a strong headline, short intro, and visible CTA above the fold
- Structure pages clearly: hero, benefits, proof, CTA
- Use clear visual hierarchy with readable fonts, whitespace, and strong contrast
- Optimize for mobile responsiveness and thumb-friendly navigation
- Keep load time under 3 seconds when possible
- Use testimonials, trust badges, reviews, and proof to support conversions
- Keep forms short and easy to complete
- Run A/B tests on headlines, CTA text, and button design
- Use user behavior data like CTR, funnels, and heatmaps to improve pages

## 2. SEO / Keyword Strategy Best Practices
- Optimize content for organic search visibility, not for search engines themselves
- Match each page to one primary keyword/topic and clear search intent
- Use target keywords in title tag, meta description, URL, H1, early body copy, and image alt text
- Keep meta titles around 50–60 characters
- Keep meta descriptions under about 160 characters
- Avoid keyword stuffing and prioritize natural language
- Use internal links to connect related pages
- Use descriptive, short URL slugs
- Optimize images with descriptive file names and alt text
- Use canonical tags to manage duplicate content
- Use 301 redirects for removed or outdated pages
- Improve crawlability, indexability, page speed, mobile usability, HTTPS, and site structure
- Use local SEO tactics like Google Business Profile, reviews, and location-based keywords
- Build backlinks and authority through quality referring domains, PR, partnerships, and useful content

## 3. AEO / GEO / AI Search Best Practices
- Focus on question-based keywords and real user questions
- Structure headings as questions when relevant
- Answer questions directly in the first 1–2 sentences under a heading
- Keep direct answers concise and easy to quote
- Use bullet points, numbered lists, and modular sections
- Make each section understandable on its own
- Use consistent formatting like definition → detail → example
- Optimize content for featured snippets, People Also Ask, and AI Overviews
- Use schema markup such as FAQ, HowTo, Article, and Local Business where relevant
- Make content easy for AI systems to parse, extract, and cite
- Prioritize clarity, structure, and trust over creativity in formatting
- Add trust signals like expert insights, credentials, citations, statistics, and case studies
- Build topical authority with clusters of related content
- Keep important pages crawlable and accessible
- Update content regularly so it stays fresh and relevant
- Track AI visibility, citations, and brand mentions in addition to rankings and clicks

## 4. Social Media / Content Marketing Best Practices
- Align social media goals with broader business objectives
- Use audience personas to guide messaging and content choices
- Post consistently and prioritize content quality and relevance
- Create content that informs, entertains, inspires, or connects
- Prioritize video and strong visuals for engagement
- Focus on the first few seconds of content to stop scrolling
- Optimize captions, bios, hashtags, highlights, and thumbnails with keywords
- Track watch time, retention, engagement, profile clicks, and conversions
- Test different content formats and repeat what performs best
- Research trends, competitors, and niche content opportunities
- Use stories, reels, live content, and platform-specific formats strategically
- Encourage interaction through comments, polls, questions, and user-generated content
- Treat social as both a visibility tool and a relationship-building channel
- Use influencers, creators, UGC, paid social, retargeting, and lookalikes strategically
- Link social content back to site content where appropriate

## 5. Analytics / Digital Strategy Best Practices
- Align marketing activity with business goals and measurable KPIs
- Use data to guide content, UX, SEO, and campaign decisions
- Track acquisition, engagement, conversion, and retention metrics
- Identify drop-off points and underperforming pages or channels
- Use data for experimentation, iteration, and personalization
- Treat digital strategy as a full business system, not just a channel tactic
- Adapt quickly to trends, platform shifts, and customer behavior
- Focus on customer value, not just tools or technology
- Use experimentation and testing as part of ongoing optimization
- Build scalable strategies through systems, partnerships, and continuous improvement
